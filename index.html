About

The person behind the pixels

I believe great design starts with understanding — really understanding — the person on the other side of the screen. Not just their goals, but their fears. Not just what they need to do, but how they feel when they do it.

Over the past decade, I've worked with startups finding their voice, agencies learning to listen, and enterprises remembering what clarity feels like. The industries change. The humans don't.

When I'm not designing, I'm probably reading about how people make decisions, why stories matter, or what makes some things feel inevitable while others feel forced. All of it informs the work.
